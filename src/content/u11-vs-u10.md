# U10 to U11 — Every Rule Difference

**Source:** RFU Regulation 15, Appendix 4 (U10 Rules of Play) and Appendix 5 (U11 Rules of Play).

**Effective 1 August 2025.** Wording variations with no change in meaning are excluded.

---

## THE HEADLINE CHANGES

- **Nine a side instead of eight.**
- **Twenty minute halves instead of fifteen**, on a wider pitch (43m instead of 35m).
- **Kicking comes in.** Tactical kicking from hand is now allowed, restarts are drop kicks, and a 15m line is introduced.
- **The scrum strike is contested.** Both hookers may now strike for the ball. Still no pushing.
- **Free kick replaces free pass** as the standard sanction for most infringements.
- **Ruck and maul go up to three a side** (the ball carrier plus two), instead of two.
- **Ruck and maul "use it" failures now give a scrum**, not a free pass.

---

## 1. Match structure

### 1.1 Players per team
- **U10:** a maximum of 8 players per team on the pitch.
- **U11:** a maximum of 9 players per team on the pitch.

### 1.2 Length of each half
- **U10:** a maximum of 15 minutes per half.
- **U11:** a maximum of 20 minutes per half.

### 1.3 Pitch size
- **U10:** a maximum of 60m by 35m, plus 5m of in-goal at each end.
- **U11:** a maximum of 60m by 43m, plus 5m of in-goal at each end. The pitch is 8m wider.

### 1.4 The 15m line
- **U10:** none.
- **U11:** a line is marked 15m from each goal line. It does the same job as the 22m line in the adult game and is referred to throughout the U11 rules as the "22". It matters because kicking is now allowed and territory can be gained from it.

---

## 2. Restarts and kicking

### 2.1 Starting and restarting play
- **U10:** every restart is a free pass from the centre of the pitch. This applies to the start of each half and to restarts after a score. There is no kicking.
- **U11:** every restart is a drop kick from the centre of the halfway line, for the start of each half and after a score. The full rules are:
  - The kick must travel at least 7m. The non-kicking team must stand at least 7m back.
  - After a score, the team that conceded chooses whether to receive the kick or to kick off themselves.
  - If the kick does not reach 7m but an opponent plays it first, play continues.
  - If the kick does not reach 7m, or the kicking team plays it before 7m, or it goes directly into touch: the non-kicking team chooses either a scrum at the centre of the halfway line or a re-kick.
  - If the kicking team is in front of the ball: scrum to the non-kicking team at the centre of the halfway line (no choice).
  - If the kick goes into the in-goal untouched and is made dead, or goes into touch-in-goal: the non-kicking team chooses either a scrum at the centre of the halfway line or a re-kick.

### 2.2 Kicking in open play
- **U10:** all kicking is banned. Any kick is penalised.
- **U11:** kicking from hand is allowed, with these limits:
  - Tactical kicking from hand is allowed.
  - Kicking the ball along the ground (a "fly-hack") is not allowed.
  - Box kicks and drop goals are not allowed.

### 2.3 Kicking the ball into touch
This is new at U11 and follows the same gain-in-ground principle as the adult game.

- **Kicked into touch from outside the 15m line:** no ground is gained. The free pass to the opponents is taken level with the spot where the ball was kicked, 5m in from the touchline.
- **Kicked into touch from inside the 15m zone by the defending team:** ground is gained. The free pass to the opponents is taken level with where the ball crossed the touchline, 5m in.
- **Exception:** if the defending team had passed the ball back into the 15m zone, and there was no ruck, maul, tackle, or touch by an opponent after that, no ground is gained and the free pass is taken level with where the ball was kicked.
- In place of the free pass, the non-kicking team may take a quick throw-in, as long as it is the same ball and it has not been touched by anyone other than a player. The thrower may stand anywhere outside the field of play between the line of touch and their own goal line.

### 2.4 The mark
- **U10:** none (no kicking).
- **U11:** a player may call a mark anywhere on the pitch by catching an opponent's kick cleanly. This does not apply to a restart or a free kick. A successful mark gives that player's team a free kick.

### 2.5 Catching a kick and knocking on
- **U10:** none (no kicking).
- **U11:** if a player of the non-kicking team plays an opponent's kick in flight and knocks it forward, the non-kicking team is awarded a scrum where the ball was touched. If they play it and it goes backward, advantage applies and, if none is gained, the non-kicking team gets a scrum. In effect the team trying to catch is not penalised for a knock-on, which encourages players to attempt the catch.

### 2.6 Drop-out at the "22"
- **U10:** none.
- **U11:** a drop-out at the 15m line is awarded when:
  - the ball is kicked into the in-goal by the attacking team and made dead by a defender; or
  - the ball is kicked, sent, or carried into the in-goal by the attacking team and then touches or crosses the touch-in-goal or dead ball line.

### 2.7 Offside at a kick
- **U10:** none (no kicking).
- **U11:** a player is offside if they are in front of the ball when it is kicked, or within 7m of an opponent who is waiting to play the ball or waiting where the ball will land.

## 3. Sanctions

This is the single biggest change in how the game is refereed. At U10 almost every infringement is punished with a free pass. At U11 almost every infringement is punished with a free kick.

### 3.1 What each restart is
- **Free pass (both age grades):** the passer holds the ball in both hands and, on the referee's call of "Play", passes it backwards through the air. No player may run until the pass is made. Opponents stand 7m back. The ball cannot be kicked from a free pass.
- **Free kick (U11 only):** a kick from the hand that moves the ball a visible distance, either a tap to oneself or a kick to gain ground. Opponents stand 7m back. Because it can be kicked, it opens up tactical options a free pass does not.

At U11 the free pass survives in only one situation: when the ball or ball carrier goes into touch.

### 3.2 Sanction for each infringement

| Infringement | U10 | U11 |
|---|---|---|
| Foul play | Free pass | Free kick |
| Offside | Free pass | Free kick |
| Hand-off or fend-off | Free pass | Free kick |
| Squeezeball | Free pass | Free kick |
| High tackle (contact above the base of the sternum) | Free pass | Free kick |
| Pushing in the scrum | Free pass | Free kick |
| Voluntarily falling on a grounded player | Not listed at U10 | Free kick (new at U11) |
| Ruck: ball not used within 5 seconds of "Use it" | Free pass | Scrum |
| Maul: ball not used within 5 seconds of "Use it" | Free pass | Scrum |
| Ball or ball carrier into touch | Free pass | Free pass (unchanged) |

### 3.3 New infringement: falling on a grounded player
U11 adds an infringement that U10 does not list: voluntarily falling on or over a player who is on the ground with the ball. A player is assumed to have fallen on purpose unless the referee is certain it was accidental. In the rare case where it is accidental, play stops and a scrum is given to the team that had the ball.

### 3.4 Advantage at listed infringements
Both age grades keep the general principle that advantage should be played whenever it is safe to do so. U11 adds one note that U10 does not have: at the specific infringements listed in its infringements section (such as foul play, hand-off, squeezeball, falling on a grounded player, contact off the ball, and pushing in the scrum), no advantage is played.

### 3.5 High tackle: referee procedure
- **U10:** for contact above the base of the sternum, the rule requires the referee to stop the game, speak to the offender, and remind them of the contact rules, then award the restart.
- **U11:** the high tackle is simply listed as an infringement carrying a free kick. The stop-and-remind step is not written into the U11 rule.

## 4. Scrum

### 4.1 The contested strike
- **U10:** the scrum is uncontested. Only the team putting the ball in may strike for it. Neither team may push.
- **U11:** both hookers may strike for the ball. Neither team may push. This is the headline scrum change at U11.

### 4.2 When a scrum is awarded
- **U10:** for a forward pass or a knock-on only.
- **U11:** for a forward pass or a knock-on, and also:
  - where the ball does not come out of a ruck or maul; or
  - where the ball becomes unplayable.

At U10 those last two situations give a free pass instead.

### 4.3 Feeding the scrum
- **U10:** no feeding rule (only one team can strike, so a straight feed does not matter).
- **U11:** because both hookers can now strike, the rules add a referee instruction on a straight feed. The scrum half must hold the ball in both hands, its long axis parallel to the ground and the touchline, between knee and ankle height, and release it so it lands midway between the two front rows and beyond the near prop's shoulders.

### 4.4 Scrum offside line
- **U10:** not stated as an offside line. The back line must simply stay at least 5m behind the scrum until the ball is out.
- **U11:** a player is offside if they are less than 5m from the scrum before the ball is out, unless they are the scrum half or part of the scrum.

## 5. Tackle, ruck and maul

### 5.1 Supporting players at the tackle
- **U10:** one supporting player from each team may act at the tackle (rip and pass, pick up and pass, pick up and run, or join a ruck).
- **U11:** two supporting players from each team may do so.

### 5.2 Players allowed in a ruck
- **U10:** no more than two players from either team in the ruck, counting the ball carrier and opponent. That is the ball carrier plus one team mate per side. The U10 rules describe this as a 1 against 1 contest.
- **U11:** no more than three players from either team in the ruck. That is the ball carrier plus two team mates per side. The U11 rules describe this as a 2 against 2 contest.

### 5.3 Players allowed in a maul
- **U10:** no more than two players from either team in the maul.
- **U11:** no more than three players from either team in the maul.

### 5.4 Offside line at the tackle
- **U10:** there is a single offside line at the tackle, set at the hindmost foot of the hindmost player of each team.
- **U11:** there are two offside lines, one at the hindmost point of the tackled player and one at the tackler, as in the adult game.

### 5.5 Extra defender restriction at the ruck
- **U10:** the ruck offside rule adds that defenders must stay between their own goal line and the tackled player until the pass is made.
- **U11:** this extra sentence is not included. The two offside lines at the hindmost point of the ruck apply, as in the adult game.

---

## 6. Ball on the ground
- **U10:** no rule on this.
- **U11:** a new section sets out what happens when the ball is on the ground:
  - Players play on their feet with the ball in hand.
  - If the ball is on the ground, players are encouraged to pick it up. A player who dives on it must get up with it, pass it, or release it at once, and be allowed to do so.
  - If the ball is knocked forward, a scrum is given to the other team unless advantage applies.
  - If the ball is passed backwards and goes to ground, play continues and either team may pick it up. If it rolls into touch, the other team gets a free pass 5m in from the touchline.

---

## 7. Carried over unchanged

These points are the same at both age grades and are worth confirming for a coach moving up:
- Size 4 ball.
- The tackle must be below the base of the sternum, with arms used.
- No hand-off or fend-off by the ball carrier.
- Squeezeball is banned, and may not be coached.
- No pushing in the scrum.
- The scrum is the nearest three players, with the fourth acting as scrum half. The scrum half must pass from the base and may not run.
- Rolling substitutions, with players able to return.
- Coaches stay off the pitch while play is live.
- The match ends if the try difference goes above six (RFU Regulation 15.11.3).
